# 6. Change Management

## Adoption is a behaviour change

A technically sound AI tool can fail when employees do not trust it, managers do not reinforce it, or the workflow does not fit daily work. Adoption therefore requires coordinated changes in skills, incentives, process and support.

## Stakeholder map

| Stakeholder | Primary need |
|---|---|
| Regional leadership | Value, risk and portfolio visibility |
| Business heads | Workflow outcomes and accountable ownership |
| Risk, legal and privacy | Clear boundaries and evidence |
| Technology | Integration, resilience and support model |
| HR and learning | Role-based capability development |
| Local markets | Regulation, language and process localisation |
| Managers | Team expectations and performance guidance |
| End users | Practical examples, confidence and help channels |

## Change plan

### Prepare

- define the case for change;
- identify affected roles and workflows;
- assess concerns and readiness;
- recruit sponsors and local champions;
- establish baseline measures.

### Enable

- provide role-based training rather than generic awareness;
- teach users when not to use AI;
- publish approved workflow patterns;
- provide office hours, FAQs and escalation routes;
- equip managers to coach responsible use.

### Reinforce

- share verified outcomes and lessons;
- recognise safe, useful adoption rather than raw usage;
- monitor confidence, errors and workarounds;
- update training and controls from feedback;
- retire ineffective use cases.

## Champion network

Champions should:

- translate regional standards into local practice;
- demonstrate approved workflows;
- collect friction and feedback;
- identify policy ambiguity;
- support users without becoming informal approvers.

## Communications rhythm

- launch message from accountable leadership;
- practical weekly tips during pilots;
- monthly outcome and risk updates;
- transparent incident and lesson sharing;
- regular reminders that human accountability remains.

## Resistance handling

Treat resistance as evidence. Concerns may reveal poor workflow fit, unclear accountability, inadequate training, real job-impact anxiety or legitimate risk. The response should be dialogue and redesign, not pressure to increase usage.
