# 2. Adoption Strategy

## Principle

AI adoption should progress from a clearly defined business problem to a controlled pilot, then scale only when value, usability and risk controls have been demonstrated.

## Four-wave approach

### Wave 1 — Discover

- interview business, technology, risk and operations stakeholders;
- map high-friction workflows;
- assess AI literacy and readiness;
- identify measurable baselines;
- classify data and regulatory constraints;
- build a prioritised use-case backlog.

### Wave 2 — Pilot

- select low-to-moderate-risk, high-frequency workflows;
- define owners, users, controls and success criteria;
- start with small user cohorts;
- keep human review explicit;
- collect qualitative and quantitative feedback;
- stop pilots that do not demonstrate value.

### Wave 3 — Adopt

- redesign the workflow rather than merely adding a chatbot;
- provide role-based training and examples;
- establish AI champions and office hours;
- publish approved patterns and escalation routes;
- track sustained use, quality and trust.

### Wave 4 — Scale

- standardise reusable components;
- localise for market regulation, language and process;
- integrate outcome reporting into management routines;
- monitor incidents, drift and policy exceptions;
- continuously retire weak or obsolete use cases.

## Use-case prioritisation

A practical score should combine:

- business value;
- user pain and frequency;
- adoption feasibility;
- data readiness;
- sponsorship strength;
- regional reusability;
- measurement availability;
- implementation effort;
- legal, privacy and model risk.

High-value use cases with no safe data path should not advance until the data boundary is resolved.

## Portfolio management

The adoption portfolio should contain a mix of:

- quick productivity improvements;
- workflow-quality improvements;
- knowledge-access use cases;
- decision-support use cases with strong human oversight;
- foundational governance and capability-building work.

The portfolio should not be judged by the number of pilots launched. It should be judged by sustained outcomes and responsible scale.
