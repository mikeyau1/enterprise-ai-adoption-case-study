# 8. Measurement Framework

## Measure outcomes, not licences

AI-adoption reporting should distinguish availability, usage, sustained behaviour, quality, risk and business value.

## KPI framework

| Dimension | Example measures |
|---|---|
| Reach | Eligible users, activated users, trained users |
| Adoption | Weekly active users, approved workflows used |
| Depth | AI-assisted workflow frequency and complexity |
| Retention | 30-, 60- and 90-day sustained use |
| Capability | Training completion, assessment and confidence |
| Productivity | Cycle-time reduction and capacity released |
| Quality | Error, rework and escalation rates |
| Trust | User confidence, override rate and feedback |
| Risk | Incidents, exceptions, blocked actions and data violations |
| Value | Hours saved, cost avoided, revenue enabled or service improvement |
| Scale | Markets, functions and reusable components adopted |

## Measurement design

Every pilot should define:

- a pre-pilot baseline;
- a comparison period or control where practical;
- the unit of work being measured;
- quality and risk guardrails;
- who owns data collection;
- how self-reported benefits are validated;
- when the use case will be reviewed or retired.

## Leading and lagging indicators

### Leading

- training attendance;
- successful first workflow;
- manager participation;
- champion activity;
- feedback volume;
- unresolved access or policy blockers.

### Lagging

- sustained adoption;
- cycle-time improvement;
- quality change;
- economic value;
- incidents;
- employee or customer experience.

## Benefit calculation

Illustrative productivity benefit:

```text
Validated hours released
= eligible transaction volume
× measured time reduction per transaction
× sustained adoption rate
× quality-adjustment factor
```

Do not equate generated text volume or prompt count with value.

## Dashboard principles

- show denominators and baselines;
- separate pilot results from scaled results;
- identify assumptions;
- include quality and risk beside productivity;
- allow regional and local-market views;
- highlight inactive and retired use cases, not only successes.
