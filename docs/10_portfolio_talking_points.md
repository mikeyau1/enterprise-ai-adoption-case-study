# 10. Portfolio Talking Points

## 90-second overview

I began with an AI-assisted research repository and recognised that ad hoc prompting would not scale safely. I therefore designed a governed multi-agent operating model with clearly separated roles for repository preflight, evidence collection, validation and independent review.

The agents were read-only, the parent process remained the only writer, and every material change required human approval. I introduced task contracts, exact evidence boundaries, fail-closed escalation, audit artifacts and rollback procedures.

The main lesson was that enterprise AI adoption is not primarily a model-selection problem. It is an operating-model and change-management problem: identifying valuable workflows, assigning accountability, controlling data, supporting users and proving outcomes. This public case study translates those lessons into a reusable framework for regulated organisations operating across Asian markets.

## Evidence demonstrated by the case study

- multi-agent workflow design;
- role-based permissions;
- human-in-the-loop approval;
- deterministic validation;
- responsible-AI controls;
- adoption and value measurement;
- regional-core and local-market scaling;
- change-management planning;
- honest limits and non-production claims.

## Interview questions this project can support

### How would you prioritise AI use cases?

Explain the balance of business value, workflow frequency, adoption feasibility, data readiness, sponsorship, measurement and risk.

### How would you drive adoption across Asia?

Describe the regional-core/local-market model, phased rollout, champion network, local regulation and language adaptation.

### How would you govern generative AI?

Discuss explicit data boundaries, separation of duties, human authority, independent validation, monitoring, incident routes and fail-closed behaviour.

### How would you measure value?

Distinguish reach and usage from sustained adoption, quality-adjusted productivity, risk and validated economic outcomes.

### What did not work?

Highlight the limitations of ad hoc prompting, the cost of excessive governance, the need for clean baselines and the importance of stopping unsupported work.

## Claims to avoid

Do not describe this repository as:

- an enterprise production deployment;
- organisation-wide adoption evidence;
- a proven financial or trading system;
- a quantified productivity programme;
- work completed for a named employer.

Present it as a designed and implemented portfolio case study grounded in a real governed AI-assisted development workflow.
